Sir Tim Berners-Lee

Sir Timothy John Berners-Lee, also known as TimBL is a British computer Scientist. He was born on London. A graduate from Oxford Universtity. After graduating he became a software engineer at CERN. Inventor of World Wide Web, HTML, HTTP, director of W3C, founder of the World Wide Web Foundation.

World Wide Web

A global information medium which users can read and write via computers connected to the Internet. As often, people mistakenly compare internet and the WWW as the same, but World Wide Web is different. The Web is a service that operates over the Internet.

In 1989, A British Scientist at CERN named Sir Tim Berners-Lee invented the World Wide Web. The original idea of the web is to meet the demands for the automatic information sharing between scientists in Universities and other isntitutes all around the world.

World Wide Consortium

In September 1994, Berners-Lee founded the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology with support from the Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency(DARPA) and the European Commission. It comprised various companies that were willing to create standards and recommendations to improve the quality of the Web
